When buying a mattress,
You don't always get what you pay for

IF IT'S MORE EXPENSIVE THEN IT MUST BE SUPERIOR?

Price doesn't always guarantee better quality, longer life span, or even customer satisfaction. The only thing paying a higher price guarantees is that you spent more money.

The Truth Behind An Expensive Mattress

Big-brand mattresses (Tempur-pedic, Sealy, Serta) have expensive price tags largely because of factors that have nothing to do with actually making a mattress.

Are we saying that our Icon Sleep Mattress that costs thousands of dollars less is better than mattresses from the big name brands? NO.

What we are saying is spending thousands of dollars on a few pieces of foam with a cover on top doesn't give you any peace of mind.

Don't take our word for it, read what real customers have had to say about their mortgage payments. Oops, sorry, meant mattress purchases.

"Bought a Sealy mattress worth $1500. After about 6 months it noticeably sagged and became extremely uncomfortable."

Marc of Las Vegas. NV.

"Spent over $1800 on a Sealy mattress. In less than 3 months, we could notice a decrease in support, which is getting worse over time."

Pat of Clarkson, MI

"We purchased a Simmons mattress for $2354. After 3 months of sleeping on it, there was already a sinkhole on one side."

Julie of Fort Piece, FL

"After spending $3000 on a Tempur-Pedic mattress, I'm waking up with deep soreness and pain in my legs and stiffness in my back."

Toni of Clarksburg, PA

*All reviews quoted are from www.consumeraffairs.com

While the majority of our customers (and yes even the big name brands too) love their mattress, there is always going to be a chance you are not completely satisfied months or years down the line no matter who you buy from (as the above reviews confirm).

So the question becomes; would you rather take a chance on a $5,000 mattress from one of the aforementioned giants or a $500 mattress from a little guy like us?
